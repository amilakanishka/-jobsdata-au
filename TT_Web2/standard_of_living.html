Health, Wealth and Happiness

Key Headlines

The Index of Socio-Economic Disadvantage is an Australian Bureau of Statistics relative ratio across Australia. The Index uses a combination of economic and the social conditions of people & households within areas.

Crashes by LGA IRSD

In metro areas, the higher the Index, the less crashes. In regional, Geelong is the outlier.

IRSD by Person

The outliers are in the high country areas of Victoria. Conclusion here is the dangerous driving conditions.

Median Income

The higher the median income, the lower the instances of crashes, in metro. But the opposite in regional areas.

Metropolitan Health

In Metro Melbourne, there is a correlation between health and the number of crashes. But this does not correlate in regional areas.
